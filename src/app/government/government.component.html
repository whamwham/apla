Let’s make digital society a reality

e-Government on blockchain increases Foreign Direct Investment, raises citizens’ overall welfare, making government services work faster.

Examples of services for e-goverment

Digital identity

Ensuring all citizens have a digital ID isn’t just the foundation of e-government, but it will also lead to increased efficiency and, therefore, satisfaction from citizens interacting with government services.

Business licence issuance

Businesses of all sorts need all kinds of licenses to operate. With paper documents, storage takes up space and the documents become easily disorganized, whilst this is completed rapidly with blockchain-based digital solutions.

Land registry

Registering land in the name of an entity or citizen can take an extended period of time and corruption can mire the process, meaning it can drag on for months. With e-government, this process is transparent and efficient.

Vehicle registry

Registering a vehicle can be a long and arduous process with documents becoming lost and problems arising with regards to vehicle ownership. By using immutable blockchain-based solutions, owners can be sure of the full vehicle history.

Contract management

Many agreements between counterparties need to be registered with the government for their validity to be confirmed. Blockchain simplifies this process by making document management digital and network based.

Supply chain

Governments have their own supply chain problems that are more complicated by the bureaucracy of the system being so large. Reducing bureaucracy and going paperless will eliminate many of these problems.

Education system

Attestations are currently issued in paper format with many institutions holding copies, in addition to the certificate recipient. However, these documents can be easily falsified and their validity cannot be checked as information is kept in data silos. Distributed networks therefore reduce the opaque nature of the system.

Digital voting

Ballot box stuffing and political apathy are big issues for democratic systems. Blockchain-based voting systems make it simpler to cast ballots, whilst voting data is chronologically stored and can be verified where necessary.

Asset tokenization

Any property titles held by citizens can be entered as a registry onto the blockchain, making any asset transfers that subsequently take place more efficient and easier to tax due to the heightened transparency of the system.

Wallets for financial transactions

Citizens can hold funds digitally in so called wallets, and these funds can be used to conduct financial transactions with the state, for example: paying fines or settling tax debts before the state.

Why should you use blockchain?

Secure data

Blockchain guarantees that any information can not be arbitrarily amended, altered or or deleted by anybody except those via the democratic vote of the ecosystem community.

Transparent

It is easy to track actions made by every system user and define who is specifically responsible for each.

Speed

Operational efficiency is increased hundreds of times.
