Summary

As a national financial regulator, Luxembourg's Commission de Surveillance du Secteur Financier (CSSF) has always been friendly for the fintech innovations. In 2016 the country became the first in Europe to grant a state license to a cryptocurrency exchange.

A year later, the CSSF was ready to explore the possibilities of using blockchain for selling traditional financial instruments. A european company that develops innovative financial products met Apla to deliver a comprehensive solution.

In just 24 hours, Apla's team developed a system for banks to sell financial instruments to investors and verified it as a Proof-of-Concept. Our application integrated financial sector professionals, CSSF revisors and investors into a single safe, reliable and 100% transparent network.

Background

Traditionally, the financial service industry relies on paper ledgers. This solution is time-consuming, expensive and insecure. Nevertheless, all the key operations, such as emitting financial instruments, their approval by national financial operator and selling to financial sector professionals, are still paper-based.

Blockchain could bring certain advantages to the financial sector across various innovative projects.

CSSF

Problems

  • Lack of transparency during polls or surveys
  • Lack of trust amongst citizens towards research organizations
  • Paper documents are vulnerable: securities can be lost or falsified
  • The issuer must inform the CSSF at least five days before it intends to submit an application for approval
  • It can take up to a month for the CCSF to approve financial instruments
  • Companies admitting securities also face delays due to inherent mistakes and spend money on re-application

All solved by one solution: Apla

Solution

  1. Financial sector professionals (FSPs) and investors receive access to the app via the financial regulator (CSSF).
  2. The FSP issuing the financial instrument sends it to the CSSF for approval.
  3. Approval time is decreased as the app excludes security issues and paperwork.
  4. Financial instruments approved by CSSF are transferred to a special register.
  1. Investors can buy the financial instruments with 100% confidence.
  2. The CSSF can see all the transactions with perfect transparency of securities trading.
  3. Each change of the record in the shareholders register, notification, etc. represents one single transaction in
