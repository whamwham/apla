Summary

Entrepreneurs are an important driver of progress. But starting a new business is always complicated: it requires notarizing documents, opening bank accounts, paying fees and much more. In the Emirate of Ajman (UAE), registering with local federal authorities includes applying paper documents to 4 to 5 different government services. It takes up to 10 working days and requires tons of paperwork.

In April 2017, the administration of Ajman focused on helping businesses thrive in the economy using blockchain. Apla created a solution where every step of the licensing process can be completed digitally, at least 50% faster and easier than before. It also provides automatic data exchange between different departments and the highest level of security and transparency of all the administrative work.

Background

The Ajman Emirate (UAE) is going through a powerful shift to investment diversification. A variety of new businesses have sprung up, especially in manufacturing, retail and business services. Still, a lot of the application and registration process happens offline. Applicants have to prepare thoroughly in advance to understand every step of the process which takes time and effort.

Problems

  • Paper documents are outdated and insecure
  • Centralized databases are insecure and vulnerable to hacking
  • Applying for a license takes up to 10 working days
  • The licensee has to visit up to 6 different departments
  • No cross-department digital exchange of data

All solved by one solution: Apla

Challenge

  • Create a blockchain app for the issuance of business licenses.
  • Make visiting the authorities unnecessary.
  • Get rid of huge paper registers and tons of paperwork.
  • Ensure 100% safe and transparent transactions.
  • Facilitate communication between different departments.
  • Make getting licensed much faster and easier!

Solution

  1. The licensee creates an account and fills in the application form. From then on, they can check the overall progress at any time.
  2. General Directorate of Residency and Foreign Affairs Ajman (GDRFA) checks the shareholder names.
  3. The Ajman Department of Economic Development (ADED) waste solutions provider and Ajman Sewerage Company define whether the property is suitable for the specified type of business.
  1. The police department checks the safety of commercial lease.
  2. The Ministry for Economic Development checks the application and decides whether the license should be issued.
  3. The applicant gets the license.
