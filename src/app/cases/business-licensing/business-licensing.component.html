Summary

Entrepreneurs are an important driver of progress. But starting a new business is always complicated: it requires notarizing docs, opening bank accounts, paying fees and much more. In Ajman emirate (UAE) registering with local federal authorities includes applying paper documents to 4 to 5 different government services. It takes up to 10 working days and requires tons of paperwork.

In April 2017, Administration of Ajman focused on helping businesses thrive in the economy using blockchain. Apla created a solution where every step of licensing can be completed digitally, at least 50% faster and easier than before. It also provides automatic data exchange between different departments and the highest level of security and transparency of all the administrative work.

Background

Adman emirate (UAE) is going through a powerful shift to investment diversification. A variety of new businesses appeared, especially in manufacturing, retail and business services. Still, a lot of the application and registration process happens offline. The applicants have to prepare thoroughly in advance to understand every step of the process which takes time and effort.

Problems

  • Paper documents are outdated and insecure
  • Centralized databases are insecure and vulnerable for hacking
  • Applying for a license takes up to 10 working days
  • The licensor has to visit up to 6 different departments
  • No cross-department digital exchange of data

All solved by one solution: Apla

Challenge

  • Create a blockchain app for issuance of business licenses.
  • Make visiting the authorities unnecessary.
  • Get rid of huge paper registers and tons of paperwork.
  • Ensure 100% safe and transparent transactions.
  • Facilitate communication between different departments.
  • Make getting licensed much faster and easier!

Solution

  1. The licensor creates an account and fills in the application form. From now, he can check overall progress at any time.
  2. General Directorate of Residency and Foreign Affairs Ajman (GDRFA) is checking shareholders names.
  3. Ajman Department of Economic Development (ADED), Waste Solutions Provider and Ajman Sewerage Company define if the property is suitable for specified kind of business.
  1. Police department checks safety of commercial lease.
  2. Ministry of Economy checks the application and decides if the license should be issued.
  3. The applicant gets the license.
